# Evidence Topology Classes

Omega Infinity models translational reasoning as topology structures across biomedical evidence layers.

Each topology represents a reproducible evidence propagation pattern.

---

## 1. Cross-layer conflict

Definition:

Mechanistic or genetic evidence supports a hypothesis, but clinical evidence contradicts it.

Example:

LRRK2 inhibition improves Parkinsonian models but clinical trials fail to confirm benefit.

Interpretation:

High mechanistic plausibility with failed human translation.

---

## 2. Surrogate disconnect

Definition:

Biomarker improvement occurs without clinical outcome improvement.

Example:

Amyloid reduction without cognitive benefit in Alzheimer's disease trials.

Interpretation:

Surrogate endpoint does not propagate to patient-level benefit.

---

## 3. Durability instability

Definition:

Initial intervention signal exists but disappears over time.

Example:

Early tumor shrinkage without survival improvement.

Interpretation:

Effect is transient rather than causal.

---

## 4. Direction ambiguity

Definition:

Genetic evidence supports both activation and inhibition hypotheses depending on context.

Example:

Context-dependent pathway modulation effects.

Interpretation:

Mechanism direction unresolved.

---

## 5. Translational constraint

Definition:

Mechanistic and genetic evidence align but clinical validation is incomplete or absent.

Example:

Strong pathway biology with limited human intervention trials.

Interpretation:

Translation gap rather than contradiction.
